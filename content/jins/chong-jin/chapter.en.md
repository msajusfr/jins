## 冲劲 · chōng jìn

### The Penetrating Force

---

> Like water breaking through the dike.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Chōng Jìn

Chōng means to rush, to charge, to traverse, to pierce.

Chōng Jìn is a linear force that passes through the obstacle without stopping at its surface. It does not strike the opponent; it passes through him.

The masters describe it as:

· a wave that does not break upon the target but surpasses it,

· a single direction, without deviation,

· an energy that continues beyond the point of impact.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Intention surpasses the target

The gaze and the intention do not stop at the opponent's surface. They aim at a point beyond. The force follows the intention and passes through.

2\. The line is pure

Chōng Jìn tolerates no curve, no hesitation, no change of direction. It is the incarnation of the straight line.

3\. The entire body advances as a single mass

Chōng Jìn engages the whole body in a coordinated translation. Nothing remains behind. The movement is that of a door closing, of a wall advancing.

4\. The root stays behind

Paradox: the force traverses forward, but the root remains anchored backward. Without this double direction, Chōng Jìn becomes a mere unbalanced push.

---

## Final Summary

Chōng Jìn is the force of traversal. It does not strike the surface; it surpasses it. It does not emit toward the opponent; it emits through him.

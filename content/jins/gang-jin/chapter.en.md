## 刚劲 · gāng jìn

### The Hard Force

---

> Like the diamond that scratches everything.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Gāng Jìn

Gāng means hard, rigid, inflexible, strong.

Gāng Jìn is the force of pure hardness, of impact without compromise. It does not bend, does not retreat, does not negotiate. It strikes.

The masters describe it as:

· lightning that falls,

· the hammer that crushes,

· the will that knows no obstacle.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Hardness is a quality of the instant

Gāng Jìn is not a permanent state. It appears at the moment of impact, then dissolves. It is pure yang, the explosion that does not last.

2\. Hardness is born of softness

True Gāng Jìn does not come from prior hardness. It is born of total release, which suddenly condenses into absolute density.

3\. The impact is total

Gāng Jìn holds nothing back. It gives everything in an instant. All the force of the body, the ground, the intention, concentrates in a single point.

4\. Intention is inflexible

Even before movement, the intention is already hard. It does not doubt. It does not retreat. It traverses.

---

## Final Summary

Gāng Jìn is the force of the absolute. It knows neither compromise nor hesitation. It strikes like lightning, total and instantaneous, then returns to the silence from which it came.

## 裂劲 · liè jìn

### The Tearing Force

---

> Like cloth torn in two hands.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Liè Jìn

Liè means to tear, to split, to separate.

Liè Jìn is a force of brutal separation. Two hands or two forces pull in opposite directions, tearing the opponent's structure.

The masters describe it as:

· paper torn in two,

· prey contested by two predators,

· a force that divides the opponent's unity.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The separation is opposed

Liè Jìn applies two equal and opposite forces. The opponent is torn between two incompatible directions.

2\. The tear is instantaneous

Liè Jìn does not pull progressively. It tears in one sharp movement, like breaking a stick over the knee.

3\. The entire body provides both forces

The two hands do not work alone. Each force comes from one side of the body, anchored and turned.

4\. The opponent's unity is broken

The aim of Liè Jìn is to separate the opponent's structure into two parts that can no longer support each other.

---

## Final Summary

Liè Jìn is the force of division. It does not strike a point; it separates two parts. It tears the opponent's structure by imposing contrary directions that cannot coexist.

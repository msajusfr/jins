## 放劲 · fàng jìn

### The Releasing Force

---

> Release without holding back.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Fàng Jìn

Fàng means to release, to let go, to set free.

Fàng Jìn is the moment of emission. It is not a push. It is a total letting-go of accumulated energy.

The masters describe it as:

· the bird released from the hand,

· the arrow leaving the bowstring,

· the wave giving itself to the shore.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. To release is not to push

Pushing implies continuous effort. Releasing is an abandonment. The force is held, then suddenly it is no longer held. It departs on its own.

2\. The release is total

Fàng Jìn keeps nothing in reserve. All that has been accumulated departs at once. The body empties itself completely of its energy.

3\. Release follows immediately

After the release, the body returns to calm instantaneously. No tension persists. The void already attracts the next accumulation.

4\. The right moment

Fàng Jìn demands sensing the exact instant when the release can occur. Neither too early, nor too late. It is the ripe fruit that detaches itself.

---

## Final Summary

Fàng Jìn is the art of letting go. It does not force the exit; it opens the door. The energy, long contained, launches itself on its own, because it was ready.

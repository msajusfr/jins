## 掤劲 · péng jìn

### The Expansive Force

---

> Like an inflated ball that cannot be pressed down.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Péng Jìn

Péng means to support, to bulge, to expand outward.

Péng Jìn is the fundamental expansive force. It is the first of the eight Jin of Tai Ji Quan. Without it, no other force can function.

The masters describe it as:

· a sphere inflated from within,

· a multidirectional expansion,

· the manifestation of vital energy in structure.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The expansion is spherical

Péng Jìn does not push only forward. It extends in all directions: front, back, above, below, sides. It is a voluminous presence.

2\. The force comes from within

It is not a muscular push. It is an expansion that rises from the center and inflates the body like a balloon.

3\. Elasticity is the primary quality

Péng Jìn is never hard. It is elastic, alive, resilient. It can be compressed, but it always returns. It cannot be broken.

4\. Without Péng Jìn, everything collapses

If Péng Jìn is absent, the body becomes flaccid or rigid, and all other forces lose their support. Péng Jìn is the mother of all forces.

---

## Final Summary

Péng Jìn is the energy of life in combat. It does not strike, does not push, does not control. It is the expansive presence that makes everything else possible. Without it, nothing holds.

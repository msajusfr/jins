## 吞劲 · tūn jìn

### The Swallowing Force

---

> The sea swallows the river without a sound.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Tūn Jìn

Tūn means to swallow, to absorb, to draw inward.

Tūn Jìn is the force of inward absorption. It does not resist the opponent's force; it receives it, draws it inside, and makes it disappear.

The masters describe it as:

· a mouth that opens without closing,

· a void that receives without rejecting,

· a depth in which the opponent's force loses its direction.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Swallowing is not blocking

To swallow is not to stop the force. It is to open a space so large that the force enters and finds no opposite wall. It continues, but inside a space that no longer belongs to it.

2\. The body becomes a void

Tūn Jìn demands that the practitioner become hollow at the point of contact. Not soft—empty. The opponent's force enters an absence and dissipates.

3\. The root absorbs the shock

The absorption does not stop at the surface. It continues through the joints, the spine, into the ground. The earth swallows what the body has received.

4\. The opponent falls into himself

Because his force encounters no resistance, the opponent overextends. He falls forward into the void he himself has created. Tūn Jìn merely opened the door.

---

## Final Summary

Tūn Jìn is the force of the void that receives. It does not oppose; it absorbs. It does not reject; it swallows. The opponent's force enters and does not come out, because there is nothing inside to send it back.

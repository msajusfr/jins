## 靠劲 · kào jìn

### The Body-Strike Force

---

> The shoulder and back speak when the hands fall silent.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Kào Jìn

Kào means to lean against, to press against, to use the body.

Kào Jìn is the force applied with the shoulder, the back, the hip. It is the force of the body itself becoming the weapon.

The masters describe it as:

· a wall that moves,

· a trunk that falls,

· a mass that leans without striking.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The entire body becomes the tool

Kào Jìn does not localize the force in a limb. It is the entire mass of the body that leans against the opponent.

2\. Extreme proximity

Kào Jìn is the force of zero distance. When the hands can no longer act, the body itself takes over.

3\. The lean is a massive push

Kào Jìn does not strike. It leans, it weighs, it pushes with the entire mass. It is a movement of the whole, not a percussion.

4\. The root feeds the lean

The lean of the body would be nothing without anchoring in the ground. The root transmits the force of the earth through the shoulder.

---

## Final Summary

Kào Jìn is the force of the entire body. When space is lacking for the hands, the trunk, the shoulder, the back take over. It is the mountain that leans.

## 崩劲 · bēng jìn

### The Collapsing Force

---

> Like the dike giving way all at once.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Bēng Jìn

Bēng means to collapse, to crumble, to break under pressure.

Bēng Jìn is a force of sudden rupture. It does not push gradually. It maintains an apparently static pressure, then releases everything in an instant.

The masters describe it as:

· a silent accumulation,

· a sudden breaking point,

· a release that carries away everything in its path.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Pressure precedes collapse

Bēng Jìn begins with a continuous, massive pressure that does not move. The opponent feels a force against him, but perceives no immediate threat. He leans into it.

2\. The breaking point is invisible

The exact moment of release cannot be anticipated. There is no preliminary signal. The static pressure becomes explosion without transition.

3\. The release is total

When Bēng Jìn releases, it holds nothing back. All accumulated force departs at once. It is an absolute letting-go.

4\. The opponent collapses from within

It is not the body that strikes the opponent. It is the opponent's own structure that gives way, like a wall from which the keystone has been removed.

---

## Final Summary

Bēng Jìn is the art of controlled collapse. It accumulates without showing, then releases without warning. The opponent does not fall because he has been pushed, but because the ground beneath his feet has given way.

## 整劲 · zhěng jìn

### The Whole-Body Force

---

> When the entire body moves as one, nothing can resist.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Zhěng Jìn

Zhěng means whole, complete, unified, intact.

Zhěng Jìn is the force of the body in its totality. Not an arm, not a leg, not the waist alone. The entire body, organized as a single structure, emitting as a single unit.

The masters describe it as:

· a wall that advances,

· a wave in which no drop moves alone,

· a fist that contains the entire body behind it.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The body is not a collection of parts

Zhěng Jìn begins with the understanding that the body is one. The arm does not strike alone. The foot does not root alone. The spine does not turn alone. All parts move together or the force is not whole.

2\. The connection is structural, not muscular

The unity of Zhěng Jìn is not achieved by tensing everything at once. It is achieved by aligning the skeleton so that force can pass through without loss. The muscles relax; the structure connects.

3\. Intention unifies before movement

Before the body moves, the intention has already organized it. The practitioner does not think of the hand, the foot, the waist. He thinks of the whole, and the parts follow.

4\. The opponent feels a mass, not a technique

Against Zhěng Jìn, the opponent does not feel a clever trick. He feels a mass, a wall, a wave. There is no weak point to exploit because there is no separation between the parts.

---

## Final Summary

Zhěng Jìn is the force of wholeness. It is not stronger because it uses more muscles; it is stronger because it uses no muscle in isolation. The entire body becomes one fist, one root, one wave.

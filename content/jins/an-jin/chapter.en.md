## 按劲 · àn jìn

### The Pressing-Down Force

---

> Like the hand pressing a cork under water.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Àn Jìn

Àn means to press, to push down, to compress toward the ground.

Àn Jìn is a downward pressure, often applied with both hands, that compresses the opponent toward the ground.

The masters describe it as:

· a wave that falls back,

· a hand that pushes in,

· a descent that locks.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The pressure descends

Àn Jìn does not push horizontally. It pushes downward, using gravity as an ally.

2\. Both hands work together

Àn Jìn is generally bilateral. Both hands press in coordination, creating a broad, stable surface of pressure.

3\. The root supports the descent

The pressure does not come from the weight of the arms alone. It comes from the descent of the center and the anchoring in the ground.

4\. The opponent is sealed

Àn Jìn does not seek to project far away, but to lock downward. The opponent loses his vertical mobility.

---

## Final Summary

Àn Jìn is the force that descends. It does not repel the opponent; it compresses him toward the ground. It does not strike; it pushes in, calmly, irresistibly.

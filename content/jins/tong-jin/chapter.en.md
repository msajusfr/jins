## 通劲 · tōng jìn

### The Unimpeded Force

---

> When the canal is open, the water flows without effort.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Tōng Jìn

Tōng means to pass through, to circulate freely, to be open and clear.

Tōng Jìn is not a force in itself. It is the condition of all forces: the absence of obstruction in the body's channels.

The masters describe it as:

· a river without dams,

· a highway without tolls,

· an energy that travels from the ground to the fingers without encountering the slightest obstacle.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Obstruction is the disease

Any tension, any blocked joint, any held breath is an obstruction. Tōng Jìn is first a work of cleaning: eliminating blockages.

2\. Force circulates; it does not stagnate

Tōng Jìn teaches that energy must never stagnate. It must traverse constantly, from the ground to the target, without stopping in any joint.

3\. Release is the channel

Only the release of the joints permits traversal. A hard shoulder is a cork. A free hip is an open valve.

4\. Traversal is the test

If a force can pass through the entire body without being interrupted, it is correct. If it stops somewhere, that is where work must be done.

---

## Final Summary

Tōng Jìn is the health of internal energy. Before seeking special forces, the practitioner must first ensure that the path is clear. When everything is open, the simplest force becomes powerful.

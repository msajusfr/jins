## 采劲 · cǎi jìn

### The Plucking Force

---

> Like the hand that plucks the ripe fruit.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Cǎi Jìn

Cǎi means to pluck, to seize, to pull downward.

Cǎi Jìn is a brief, dry downward pull that destabilizes the opponent by suddenly removing his upper point of support.

The masters describe it as:

· a hand that pulls the branch toward the ground,

· a quick gesture that surprises,

· a sudden descent that empties the top.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The pull is vertical and sudden

Cǎi Jìn descends rapidly, without visible preparation. The opponent must not sense the pull coming.

2\. The gesture is dry

The pull does not last. It is brief, like a sharp snap downward. It does not pull for long; it rips away the support.

3\. The descent destabilizes

The opponent loses his upper point of support and topples forward and downward. His structure collapses.

4\. The entire body participates in the descent

Cǎi Jìn is not only a pull of the hands. It is a descent of the center that gives power to the gesture.

---

## Final Summary

Cǎi Jìn is the art of plucking the opponent. A brief, dry pull that removes the upper support and precipitates the fall. The fruit drops because the hand pulled, quickly and justly.

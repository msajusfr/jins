## 沾黏劲 · zhān nián jìn

### The Adhesion Force

---

> Stick as the skin sticks to the flesh.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Zhān Nián Jìn

Zhān means to touch lightly, to make contact.

Nián means to stick, to adhere.

This is not a grip. It is a living adhesion.

The masters describe it as:

· a contact that never breaks,

· a presence that follows without resisting,

· a lightness that offers no hold.

The opponent feels something upon him, but can neither seize it nor free himself from it.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Touch without weighing

The contact is so light that it triggers no defensive reaction. The opponent perceives no threat. Yet information circulates.

2\. Follow without directing

Zhān Nián Jìn does not seek immediately to control. It accompanies the opponent's movement, embraces his direction, remains in constant connection. It is a listening in motion.

3\. Never break the link

The primary quality is continuity. A rupture of contact, however slight, is a defeat of Zhān Nián Jìn. The link must remain uninterrupted like a silk thread.

4\. Release enables adhesion

Only an empty hand, without tension, can truly stick. The slightest stiffness creates a space that the opponent can exploit.

---

## Final Summary

Zhān Nián Jìn is the art of touching without being perceived, of following without constraining, of remaining present without ever weighing. It is the first condition of listening.

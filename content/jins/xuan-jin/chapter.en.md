## 旋劲 · xuán jìn

### The Vortex Force

---

> The water turns, and the ship sinks without being struck.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Xuán Jìn

Xuán Jìn is not a simple circle. It is a rotation that sucks in and expels simultaneously.

The masters describe it as:

· a spiral that draws toward the center while projecting toward the periphery,

· a movement that destabilizes by absorption rather than percussion,

· a force that empties the opponent of his center.

Xuán Jìn is the force of the vortex.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The rotation hollows out the center

Every vortex possesses an empty eye. Xuán Jìn creates a central void that draws in the opponent's force. The opponent does not feel that he is being pulled; he feels that he is falling forward, into a hollow that did not exist an instant before.

2\. Suction and expulsion are simultaneous

The vortex draws downward and rejects outward in a single movement. Xuán Jìn functions thus: the opponent is absorbed by the rotation and projected by the same rotation. He cannot distinguish the moment he is seized from the moment he is rejected.

3\. The spiral never stops

A vortex interrupted is no longer a vortex. Xuán Jìn demands absolute continuity. The slightest pause in the rotation breaks the suction effect.

4\. The entire body turns around an invisible axis

The axis is not a fixed point in space, but a vertical line that passes through the body. Around this axis, everything turns: the feet, the hips, the waist, the shoulders. What is visible is only the surface of the spiral.

---

## Final Summary

Xuán Jìn is the force of the vortex. It attracts without pulling, expels without pushing. It hollows out a void at the center and rejects to the periphery in one continuous movement.

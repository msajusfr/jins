## 柔劲 · róu jìn

### The Soft Force

---

> Water never breaks.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Róu Jìn

Róu means soft, flexible, gentle, malleable.

Róu Jìn is the force of ultimate softness. It does not resist, does not strike, does not force. It yields, embraces, circumvents, and yet it prevails.

The masters describe it as:

· water that takes all forms,

· silk that bends without ever breaking,

· wind that passes through every obstacle.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Softness is the absence of resistance

Róu Jìn never creates direct conflict. It accepts the opponent's force, absorbs it, circumvents it. There is nothing against which to struggle.

2\. Softness is total adaptability

Róu Jìn has no fixed form. It embraces the opponent's form, molds itself into his hollows, follows his lines.

3\. Softness contains power

Róu Jìn is not weak. It is potential. Like water that can become a wave, softness can at any instant become explosion.

4\. Softness triumphs over hardness

What is hard breaks. What is soft survives. Róu Jìn is the force that wins by adaptation, not by domination.

---

## Final Summary

Róu Jìn is the force of water and wind. It strikes nothing, and nothing can strike it. It passes everywhere, adapts to everything, and always ends by traversing what resists it.

## 寸劲 · cùn jìn

### The Inch Force

---

> The distance of one inch is enough.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Cùn Jìn

Cùn is a Chinese unit of measure, about the width of a thumb. It designates an extremely short distance.

Cùn Jìn is the capacity to emit devastating force across a minuscule distance, without momentum, without recoil.

The masters describe it as:

· a wave that traverses the body in a flash,

· a force that needs no space to be born,

· the ultimate proof that power does not come from distance but from coordination.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Power does not come from the path traveled

A long wind-up is an admission of internal weakness. Cùn Jìn proves that true force is born from within, not from external kinetic accumulation.

2\. The wave rises from the ground

Even across one inch, the force follows the complete path: feet, legs, waist, spine, arms, hand. The space is compressed, but the internal trajectory remains whole.

3\. The lightning release

Cùn Jìn is a jolt. A sudden release that propagates like a shock wave through the joints.

4\. Intention precedes form

Cùn Jìn demands that the intention has already arrived before the movement begins. The body merely catches up with the mind.

---

## Final Summary

Cùn Jìn is the demonstration that distance is not the source of power. One inch suffices, for one who knows how to make the wave travel from the ground to the point of impact without the slightest interruption.

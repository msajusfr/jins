## 粘连劲 · zhān lián jìn

### The Continuous Link Force

---

> A silk thread weighs nothing, yet it does not break.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Zhān Lián Jìn

Zhān: to stick, to adhere.

Lián: to link, to connect, to continue.

Zhān Lián Jìn is the next step beyond Zhān Nián Jìn. It is no longer merely a matter of adhering, but of creating a structural link.

Where Zhān Nián Jìn listens, Zhān Lián Jìn links. It establishes a connection that passes through the entire body, from the point of contact to the ground.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The link passes through the entire body

The point of contact is only the entrance. The true link extends through the joints, the fasciae, to the root in the ground. The opponent is not merely stuck to a hand; he is connected to an entire structure.

2\. The link does not constrain

Zhān Lián Jìn does not imprison. It links without tightening. The opponent feels free, yet each of his movements transmits complete information.

3\. Continuity is absolute

As with Zhān Nián Jìn, no rupture is permitted. But here, continuity is not only in the contact: it is in the transmission of force through the entire body.

4\. The link prepares control

Zhān Lián Jìn creates the conditions for control. Once the link is established, the practitioner can listen, guide, then control, without ever having to seize.

---

## Final Summary

Zhān Lián Jìn is the art of linking without attaching. It transforms a simple contact into a complete structural connection, laying the foundations for all subsequent control.

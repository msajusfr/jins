## 横劲 · héng jìn

### The Horizontal Force

---

> The river cuts the plain without climbing the slope.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Héng Jìn

Héng means horizontal, transversal, lateral.

Héng Jìn is the force that acts laterally, that cuts across the opponent's direction perpendicularly, that destabilizes from the side.

The masters describe it as:

· a wave that sweeps the bridge,

· a wind that pushes the ship onto its flank,

· a force that attacks the structure at its weakest point.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The direction is perpendicular

Héng Jìn does not confront the opponent's force head-on. It takes it from the side, where it is most vulnerable.

2\. The sweep is massive

Héng Jìn does not push at a precise point. It sweeps a broad zone, using the rotation of the body to displace laterally.

3\. The opponent's force is circumvented

Rather than resisting, Héng Jìn places itself outside the line of force and pushes laterally.

4\. Anchoring permits lateral movement

Without root, Héng Jìn destabilizes the practitioner himself. Solid anchoring permits pushing laterally without falling.

---

## Final Summary

Héng Jìn is the force of the side. It does not fight force face to face, but takes it where it has no defense: laterally. It sweeps, it displaces, it overturns by the flank.

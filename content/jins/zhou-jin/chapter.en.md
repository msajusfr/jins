## 肘劲 · zhǒu jìn

### The Elbow Force

---

> The elbow is harder than the fist.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Zhǒu Jìn

Zhǒu means elbow.

Zhǒu Jìn is the force applied with the elbow. It is not merely a striking technique, but a principle of close connection.

The masters describe it as:

· a powerful pivot,

· a weapon for short distance,

· a center of rotation and pressure.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The elbow is a transmitter

The elbow does not work alone. It transmits the force of the entire body. Its power comes from the center, not from the arm.

2\. The distance is short

Zhǒu Jìn is used when the opponent is too close for the fist to be effective. It transforms proximity into advantage.

3\. The elbow is heavy

Zhǒu Jìn uses the weight of the elbow, its natural density. It does not strike in extension; it weighs, plunges, traverses.

4\. The elbow stays connected to the body

The elbow does not move far from the center. It remains close, protected, connected to the body's mass.

---

## Final Summary

Zhǒu Jìn is the force of the elbow, dense and close. It replaces the fist when distance disappears. It does not merely strike; it weighs, it turns, it drives in.

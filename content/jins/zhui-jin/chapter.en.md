## 坠劲 · zhuì jìn

### The Falling Force

---

> Like the ripe fruit that detaches from the branch.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Zhuì Jìn

Zhuì means to fall, to drop, to collapse.

Zhuì Jìn is a controlled fall. It is not a passive collapse, but the directing of a sudden mass downward, like a pile-driver that drops.

The masters describe it as:

· a sudden descent of the entire body,

· a vertical release,

· a weight that falls without being pushed.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The fall is active

Zhuì Jìn is not a passive sagging. It is a voluntary, rapid descent that engages the entire body in a fraction of a second.

2\. The body falls as a single block

The descent does not begin with one part of the body. The whole drops simultaneously, like a weight let go. No single point leads; no single point follows.

3\. The root guides the fall

The descent is not a loss of balance. It is directed, weighted, fed by the root in the ground. The practitioner does not fall; he makes the fall into a weapon.

4\. The opponent receives a mass, not a push

Zhuì Jìn does not strike with a localized impulse. It places a sudden mass upon the opponent. The difference is that between a punch and an anvil.

---

## Final Summary

Zhuì Jìn is the art of the controlled fall. It transforms the vertical descent of the body into a weapon that crushes by mass rather than striking by impulse.

## 捋劲 · lǔ jìn

### The Yielding-Leading Force

---

> Like water following the slope.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Lǔ Jìn

Lǔ means to guide, to lead, to unwind, to accompany the movement.

Lǔ Jìn is the force that follows the opponent's force to lead it into the void. It does not resist. It accompanies and lengthens.

The masters describe it as:

· a hand that draws silk without breaking it,

· a current that carries without striking,

· a redirection that empties the force of its object.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Follow before guiding

Lǔ Jìn begins with perfect accord with the opponent's direction. It does not deviate immediately. It follows first, then gradually modifies the trajectory.

2\. The opponent's force is lengthened, never stopped

Lǔ Jìn does not block. It takes the force and stretches it, lengthens it, leads it further than intended. The opponent commits into the void.

3\. The entire body retreats as one

Lǔ Jìn is not an arm gesture. It is a retreat of the entire body, which draws the opponent forward without pulling him.

4\. The void attracts

By yielding, Lǔ Jìn creates a void that destabilizes. The opponent falls into the absence of resistance.

---

## Final Summary

Lǔ Jìn is the art of yielding without breaking. It follows the force, accompanies it, stretches it, and finally leads it where it did not want to go. It empties the attack by accepting it.

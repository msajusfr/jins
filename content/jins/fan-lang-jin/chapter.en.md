## 翻浪劲 · fān làng jìn

### The Breaking-Wave Force

---

> Like the ocean that turns upon itself.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Fān Làng Jìn

Fān means to turn over, to overturn.

Làng: wave, swell.

Fān Làng Jìn is the force of the wave that rises, overturns, and crashes. It is a complete cycle of ascent, overturning, and fall.

The masters describe it as:

· a swell that hollows out,

· a crest that overturns,

· a mass of water that falls with all its weight.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The ascent precedes the fall

Fān Làng Jìn begins with an ascension of energy from the ground, like a wave forming. Then, at the summit, the wave overturns and plunges.

2\. The movement is undulating

The spine undulates like a wave. Energy rises along the back, overturns at the top of the head, and descends along the front.

3\. The overturning is the key

The crucial moment is the overturning: the energy that was rising suddenly reverses and becomes descending. This overturning is the source of power.

4\. The wave carries everything away

Fān Làng Jìn does not strike a point. It submerges the opponent with a mass of energy that rises, overturns, and crashes.

---

## Final Summary

Fān Làng Jìn is the force of the living wave. It rises from the ground, stands up to the sky, then overturns and crashes. The opponent is not struck: he is submerged by a swell that surpasses and drowns him.

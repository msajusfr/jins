## 挤劲 · jǐ jìn

### The Converging Compression Force

---

> Two forces that meet at a single point.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Jǐ Jìn

Jǐ means to squeeze, to press, to compress, to push while tightening.

Jǐ Jìn is a compression that converges toward a single point. Two forces approach and create a concentrated pressure.

The masters describe it as:

· a vise that closes,

· a wave that narrows into a jet,

· a pressure born of convergence.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Two converging forces

Jǐ Jìn arises from the meeting of two directions: often one hand supports the other, or the back pushes the arm. The resulting force emerges from their union.

2\. The pressure is punctual

All energy concentrates on a small surface. Dispersion is the enemy of Jǐ Jìn.

3\. The center advances

The compression is not only in the arms. The center of the body advances, and this forward movement gives power to the compression.

4\. The opponent is expelled by pressure

Jǐ Jìn does not push like a wide wave; it compresses a point, and it is this compression that displaces the opponent.

---

## Final Summary

Jǐ Jìn is the force of convergence. Two flows meet at a point, and from their union a pressure is born that moves everything.

## 竖劲 · shù jìn

### The Vertical Force

---

> The tree grows straight; the root descends straight.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Shù Jìn

Shù means vertical, upright, straight.

Shù Jìn is the force that acts vertically, upward or downward. It is the axis of the body, the line that connects heaven and earth.

The masters describe it as:

· a bamboo that rises and roots,

· a column that traverses the body,

· the axis around which everything else turns.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Verticality is the first structure

Before any horizontal or rotative force, the body must be vertical. Shù Jìn is the force of alignment.

2\. Two directions simultaneously

Shù Jìn rises toward heaven and descends toward earth at the same time. The head lifts; the feet sink.

3\. The axis is the condition of all rotations

Without Shù Jìn, no spiral is possible. The axis must be stable for the rotation to be powerful.

4\. Vertical force is the most natural

Gravity is our permanent ally. Shù Jìn merely uses this always-present force.

---

## Final Summary

Shù Jìn is the force of the axis. It rises and descends, connects heaven to earth, and provides the pivot around which all other forces can turn.

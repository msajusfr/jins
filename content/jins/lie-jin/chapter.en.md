## 挒劲 · liè jìn

### The Twisting-Splitting Force

---

> Like a cloth twisted to wring out water.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Liè Jìn

Liè means to twist, to split, to separate by rotation.

Liè Jìn is a twisting force that applies two opposite directions simultaneously, creating a tear by rotation.

The masters describe it as:

· a cloth being wrung out,

· a branch twisted until it breaks,

· a force that separates what was joined.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Two opposite directions

Liè Jìn applies a rotation in one direction above, and in the opposite direction below. Or one hand pushes while the other pulls. The twist arises from this opposition.

2\. The force is rotative

It is not a simple pull. It is a twist that tears the opponent's structure by imposing incompatible directions.

3\. The entire body spirals

The twist is not local. It engages the hips, the waist, the spine. The hands merely express a twist of the entire body.

4\. The separation is instantaneous

Liè Jìn does not tighten progressively. It twists and separates in one quick gesture, breaking the opponent's connection.

---

## Final Summary

Liè Jìn is the force of divergent twisting. It does not push in a single direction, but imposes two contrary directions that tear the opponent's structure apart.

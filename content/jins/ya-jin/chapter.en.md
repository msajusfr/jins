## 压劲 · yā jìn

### The Pressing Force

---

> Like a mountain pressing upon the plain.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Yā Jìn

Yā means to press, to compress, to weigh upon.

Yā Jìn is a continuous, massive downward pressure. It does not strike; it covers. It does not push; it compresses.

The masters describe it as:

· a blanket that smothers,

· a lid that seals,

· a weight that the opponent cannot lift because it has no edges to grasp.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The pressure is total and continuous

Yā Jìn does not strike in bursts. It applies a constant, heavy force that leaves no interval for the opponent to recover or reposition.

2\. The center descends into the opponent

The practitioner's center sinks through the point of contact into the opponent's structure. It is not the arms that press; it is the weight of the entire body directed downward.

3\. The opponent is sealed, not projected

Yā Jìn does not seek to send the opponent away. It immobilizes him, compresses him, makes his root impossible to find. He is buried under a weight he cannot identify.

4\. The breath is long and low

A high, short breath destroys the continuity of Yā Jìn. The pressure is carried by a long, calm exhalation that accompanies the descent of the center.

---

## Final Summary

Yā Jìn is the force that covers. It does not strike the opponent; it buries him under a continuous, edgeless weight that he cannot lift because it has no form to oppose.

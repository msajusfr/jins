## 吞吐劲 · tūn tǔ jìn

### The Swallowing-and-Spitting Force

---

> First drink the wine, then return the cup.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Tūn Tǔ Jìn

Tūn: to swallow, to absorb.

Tǔ: to spit, to emit, to return.

Tūn Tǔ Jìn is the complete cycle: absorption followed by emission. It is not two separate forces; it is one force with two phases.

The masters describe it as:

· the sea that rises and then withdraws,

· the bow that bends and then releases,

· the breath that inhales and then exhales.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. Swallowing prepares spitting

One cannot spit what one has not first swallowed. The absorption phase is not passive waiting; it is the accumulation that makes the subsequent emission possible.

2\. The two phases are inseparable

Tūn Tǔ Jìn is not first one, then the other. The swallowing already contains the spitting. The compression contains the expansion. The withdrawal contains the advance.

3\. The transition is invisible

The opponent feels himself being absorbed. He resists, pushes harder, commits himself. At the moment of maximum commitment, the direction reverses. What swallowed now spits. He is projected by his own force.

4\. The body stores and then releases

During the swallowing phase, the body stores energy like a spring. The more the opponent pushes, the more the spring compresses. The release uses the opponent's own energy, amplified by the practitioner's structure.

---

## Final Summary

Tūn Tǔ Jìn is the force of the complete cycle. It absorbs without keeping, emits without forcing. The opponent is first drawn in, then returned to himself with interest.

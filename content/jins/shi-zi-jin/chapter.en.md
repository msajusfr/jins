## 十字劲 · shí zì jìn

### The Cross Force

---

> Vertical and horizontal cross, and the world holds.

— Traditional saying

---

## The Essence of Shí Zì Jìn

Shí: ten, cross.

Zì: character.

Shí Zì Jìn is the combination of Shù Jìn and Héng Jìn. Vertical force and horizontal force cross at a point, creating an indestructible structure.

The masters describe it as:

· the beam and pillar that meet,

· the center of a cross where everything holds,

· the intersection of heaven-earth and horizontal space.

---

## Fundamental Principles

1\. The cross is the structure of stability

A single point crosses vertical force (up-down) and horizontal force (front-back, left-right). This point is the center.

2\. The dantian is the crossing

Shí Zì Jìn organizes around the dantian. It is there that the forces cross and balance.

3\. The cross permits all directions

When the cross is established, the practitioner can emit in any direction without losing center.

4\. The opponent meets a complete structure

Shí Zì Jìn opposes to the opponent not a single force, but an organized structure that resists in all directions.

---

## Final Summary

Shí Zì Jìn is the force of intersection. Vertical and horizontal cross at the center, creating a stable structure from which all directions become possible.
